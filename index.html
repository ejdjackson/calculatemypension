Stages of the Calculation
Accumulate Funds To Retirement
Your current fund is accumulated with your planned regular contributions and fund growth up to your chosen retirement date.
Calculate Maximum Affordable Retirment Income
Withdrawals from your fund are simulated multiple times, adjusting the payments each time in order to target a final fund of zero. The withdrawal that hits this target is your maximum affordable retirement income. If this is less than your desired retirement income, the shortfall is calculated.
Optimise Drawdown Plan for Tax Efficiency
If you have ISA funds to draw from, the split of payments between pension and ISA is calculated each year to minimise tax due.
The Six Initial Inputs
1) Your Current Age
No need to enter you date of birth. Date of birth is only required to calculate your state pension age. There is an option to input your actual birthday if this might change the calculation.
2) Desired Retirement Age
An initial value is required, but you will be able to adjust this up or down to see the effects on your affordable retirement income.
3) Current Pension Fund Value
This is the combined value of all your pension pots if you have more than one. The nearest £10k is enough accuracy to begin with, but you will be able to easily adjust it up or down.
4) Desired Retirement Income
Expressed as a monthly value after tax. This is worth taking time to figure out because it must cover all of your monthly expenses and disposable income in retirement.
5) Regular Pension Contributions
The amount that will be added to your pension fund each month. If you receive employers contributions, include these.
6) End of Projection Age
The calculator will aim to run down your funds to zero at this age. How long do you think you will need the level of income you have specified in 4)?

What if there is a shortfall at my desired retirement age?

There are three main options:

- Increase your monthly contributions.

- Retire later.

- Reduce your desired retirement income.

Adjust these inputs to try to remove the shortfall.

Does the calculator constitute financial advice?
The results of the calculator are for information purposes only and do not constitute financial advice. The calculations and projections are based on user-provided inputs and general assumptions, which may not reflect your specific circumstances or needs. The results should not be relied upon as a sole basis for financial decisions. For personalised financial advice tailored to your individual situation, please consult a qualified financial adviser.
