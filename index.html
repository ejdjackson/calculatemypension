The 6 Initial Inputs
1) Your Current Age
No need to enter you date of birth. Date of birth is only required to calculate your state pension age. There is an option to input your actual birthday if this might change the calculation.
2) Desired Retirement Age
An initial value is required, but you will be able to adjust this up or down to see the effects on your affordable retirement income.
3) Current Pension Fund Value
This is the combined value of all your pension pots if you have more than one. The nearest £10k is enough accuracy to begin with, but you will be able to easily adjust it up or down.
4) Desired Retirement Income
Expressed as a monthly value after tax. This is worth taking time to figure out because it must cover all of your monthly expenses and disposable income in retirement.
5) Regular Pension Contributions
The amount that will be added to your pension fund each month. If you receive employers contributions, include these.
6) End of Projection Age
The calculator will aim to run down your funds to zero at this age. How long do you think you will need the level of income you have specified in 4)?
Limitations of the Calculator
▶
How accurate is the calculator?
The results of the model are based on deterministic economic assumptions for fund growth and inflation. It is assumed that these are fixed and do not vary from a single value over time. In reality they will fluctuate up and down.

You can adjust the values of the assumptions in the model to see what effect changing them has on your plans. But it is important to note that the results are only a guide to help you understand what might happen under certain scenarios.
▶
Does the calculator constitute financial advice?
The results of the calculator are for information purposes only and do not constitute financial advice. The results should not be relied upon as a sole basis for financial decisions. For personalised financial advice tailored to your individual situation, please consult a qualified financial adviser.
